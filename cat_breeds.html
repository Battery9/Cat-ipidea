Bengal Cat

The Bengal cat is a domesticated breed that is a cross between the Asian leopard cat and a domesticated tabby cat. These cats are known for their distinctive coat, which is covered in leopard-like spots and has a soft, plush feel. Bengal cats are also known for their high energy and intelligence, making them a popular breed for families and pet owners who enjoy an active lifestyle.

Maine Coon Cat

The Maine Coon cat is one of the oldest breeds of domesticated cats in the United States. They are known for their large size, long hair, and friendly, playful personality. Maine Coon cats are also known for their distinctive appearance, with tufted ears and long, bushy tails.

Abyssinian Cat

With its lean, elegant profile, the Abyssinian cat bears a close resemblance to ancient Egyptian cat statues. Rich, coats in exotic shades of gold and cinnamon combine with large, expressive eyes, lending a sense of mystery to the breed's appearance.

Birman Cat

Also known as the sacred cat of Burma, the Birman cat is a large, long-haired breed with a heavy bone structure. Most of these cats exceed 12 pounds once they reach adulthood. Birman cats tend to be quiet, a bit shy with strangers, and they can be somewhat reserved even with people they know well.
